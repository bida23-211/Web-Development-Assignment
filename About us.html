About Warner Bros

Our History

Warner Bros. was founded in 1923 by four visionary brothers, Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner. The company began as a small film studio and quickly made a mark in the entertainment industry with the release of "The Jazz Singer" in 1927, the first feature-length talkie that revolutionized filmmaking.

Our Mission

At Warner Bros., our mission is to inspire and entertain audiences worldwide by creating exceptional, innovative content. We are dedicated to storytelling excellence, leveraging our rich heritage and cutting-edge technology to bring compelling narratives to life. Through our diverse and inclusive creations, we aim to connect people and cultures, enriching lives with experiences that spark imagination and joy.
